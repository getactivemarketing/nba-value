# TruLine — Product Marketing Context

*Last updated: 2026-04-06*

## Product Overview
**One-liner:** AI-powered sports betting intelligence that finds value bets and tracks every prediction with full transparency.
**What it does:** TruLine uses LightGBM machine learning models to predict NBA and MLB game outcomes, compares model probabilities against odds from 11+ sportsbooks, and surfaces the best value bets with a 0-100 Value Score. Specializes in NRFI (No Run First Inning) tracking for MLB.
**Product category:** Sports betting analytics / AI picks / value bet finder
**Product type:** Free web app (eventual freemium)
**Business model:** Free to use, no signup required. Revenue comes later via premium tier. Current focus is building a social media following through daily picks content.
**Website:** https://www.truline.app/

## Target Audience

### Primary: Casual Sports Bettors
- Bet 1-5 games per day, mostly small stakes ($5-$50)
- Follow picks accounts on Twitter/X and Instagram
- Want simple "bet this" recommendations, not complex analysis
- Follow 3-5 picks accounts, drop them when they go cold
- Care about win rate and transparency (show your record)
- Mostly bet MLB and NBA

### Secondary: Sharp/Analytical Bettors
- Have their own methods but want a second data point
- Value data-driven approaches over gut picks
- Interested in model methodology (ML, value scoring, edge calculation)
- Use Action Network, Covers, OddsJam alongside their own analysis
- Skeptical of "trust me bro" picks accounts

## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:** Sports bettors lack a systematic, data-driven edge. Most bet on gut feel, follow hype, or trust picks accounts that don't show their losses.
**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Picks accounts: Cherry-pick wins, hide losses, no methodology transparency, "trust me bro" energy
- Action Network/Covers: General tools, not focused on finding value edges specifically
- OddsJam: Good for arb/positive EV but not accessible for casual bettors
- Manual research: Evaluating pitcher matchups, park factors, weather, and odds across 15 games takes hours
**NRFI-specific pain:** Evaluating whether a game will be scoreless in the first inning requires analyzing starting pitcher ERA/WHIP/K9, team first inning scoring rates, park factors, and weather — too many variables to track manually across 15 daily games.

## Brand Voice
**Tone:** Confident but not arrogant. Data-forward. Accessible. Transparent. Never "LOCK OF THE DAY" energy.
**Style:** Lead with the data. Show the value score. Post losses as prominently as wins. Explain concepts simply.
**Personality:** The smart friend who actually tracks their bets and shows you the spreadsheet.
